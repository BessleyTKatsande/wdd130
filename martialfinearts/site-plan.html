Overview

Purpose

This site was created out of a deep love of martial arts and seeks to spread that same desire across the globe. To achieve this goal, individuals must be educated on all things involving martial arts, and this will include trying to undo any errors that may have been spread by misinformation. Unfortunately, this site will not teach people how to perform martial arts, and it is highly advised that individuals do not take any information learned on this site and apply it in a real-life fighting scenario. Ultimately, this site aims to give an individual basic information to help them decide if a certain martial art is worth practicing or not.

Audience

This site is dedicated to all those who harbor a love of or seek to know more about martial arts in general. Now, those who actually practice martial arts may greatly benefit from our resources, but we will try to be as inclusive as possible. Additionally, there is no age limit, as we believe that everyone should know a little about martial arts, and it will be even better if we inspire some to become masters of a certain style.

Content

Home page

Welcome to Martial Fine Arts. Here, we explore everything about martial arts. For starters, martial arts have diverse origins, as many countries have created their own unique forms of fighting to respond to a variety of situations ranging from armed to unarmed combat. We will also try to answer questions such as why some martial arts have their practitioners wear a gi and why certain martial arts have different colored belts. Lastly, we will also try to evaluate the lethality of a martial art and the best situations to utilize it.

Types of martial arts- Hand and foot: These forms of martial arts are dubbed as unarmed and often involve fighting while standing because they concentrate more on utilizing hand and foot strikes. Arm strikes are composed of hitting with a section of the arm, shoulder, elbow, palm, or knuckles. Examples of these martial arts include boxing and karate. Foot strikes are physical strikes made with the foot's ball, heel, shin, knee, or thigh. Examples of these martial arts include taekwondo and Muay thai. Grapple: Grappling is a full-contact combat sport based on throws, trips, sweeps, ground fighting, and submission grips. It can also be divided into stand-up and ground grappling. Examples of these martial arts include wrestling, judo, aikido, and jiu-jitsu. Weapons: These are usually called armed martial arts because they involve the use of a weapon. Sometimes bodily strikes can be used in conjunction with the weapon. Examples of these martial arts include fencing, kendo, bojutsu, and Zulu stickfighting. Weapons can be divided into several categories such as melee, ranged, flexible, and defensive. Melee weapons are usually used directly and examples include swords and knives. Ranged weapons are used from a distance and examples include the bow and arrow. Flexible weapons include the whip and nunchaku. Lastly, defensive weapons are mostly used to protect and sometimes take down assailants with minimal harm. Examples include armor and shields.
